# Cyber Realm ICS-WATER

## A 2-Hour Water-Sector Cybersecurity Tabletop Card Game Session

**Audience:** AWWA / water-sector operators, IT, OT, security, and utility leadership  
**Mode:** Small Organization Defense vs Script Kiddie Attack  
**Format:** Facilitated tabletop exercise disguised as a trading card game

---

# Slide 1 — What Is Cyber Realm ICS-WATER?

Cyber Realm ICS-WATER is a cybersecurity tabletop exercise built as a trading card game.

Players learn how cyber attacks can affect water operations and how defenders detect, respond, recover, and reinforce.

**Core idea:**

Red Team creates pressure.  
Blue Team protects the utility.  
The table learns from the scenario.

---

# Slide 2 — Why This Matters

Water utilities rely on connected systems, operators, vendors, remote access, control systems, and physical infrastructure.

A cyber event can become an operational event when it affects:

- Water availability
- Pumping operations
- Chemical treatment
- Remote monitoring
- Public trust
- Staff response time

This game turns those risks into playable tabletop decisions.

---

# Slide 3 — Session Goal

By the end of the session, players should understand:

- How simple cyber attacks begin
- Why exposed services and weak access matter
- Why water assets need cyber protection
- How defenders detect and respond
- How recovery and reinforcement reduce impact
- How tabletop exercises improve readiness

The goal is not only to win. The goal is to learn.

---

# Slide 4 — Two Teams

## Blue Team: Small Organization / Defender

Protects water operations using assets, defenses, tools, operators, protocols, and resources.

## Red Team: Script Kiddie / Attacker

Uses low-sophistication attacks, public tools, exposed services, credentials, malware, and command channels.

---

# Slide 5 — Blue Team Defense Lifecycle

Blue Team cards follow the operational defense lifecycle:

1. **Detect** — Find the problem
2. **Respond** — Stop or reduce the attack
3. **Recover** — Restore operations
4. **Reinforce** — Make the system stronger

Players should ask:  
**What would we do at a real utility?**

---

# Slide 6 — Red Team Cyber Kill Chain

Red Team cards follow the Cyber Kill Chain:

1. Recon
2. Weaponize
3. Delivery
4. Exploit
5. Install
6. Command and Control
7. Actions on Objectives

Players should ask:  
**Where is the attacker in the chain?**

---

# Slide 7 — Card Categories

Each card belongs to one category:

- Asset
- Defense
- Attack
- Tool
- Protocol
- Operator
- Realm
- Event

Important rule:

- Defenders use **Defense** cards
- Attackers use **Attack** cards
- Both sides may use support cards such as Asset, Tool, Protocol, Operator, Realm, and Event

---

# Slide 12 — Scenario: Water Tower Overflow

For the first guided game, use this scenario:

**Red Team wins** if Water Tower and Gateway are both damaged before turn 6.

**Blue Team wins** if Water Tower survives through turn 6.

This keeps the game focused on operational impact instead of abstract points.

---

# Slide 13 — How a Turn Works

Each turn has five simple phases:

1. Draw — Draw 1 card
2. Resource — Play up to 1 Realm card
3. Deploy — Play cards by paying cost
4. Conflict — Resolve attacks, defenses, and effects
5. Cleanup — Discard down to 7 cards if needed

Teach through play. Do not explain every card before starting.

---

# Slide 14 — Guided Example Turn

1. Red Team plays **NMAP**
2. Facilitator explains: this is reconnaissance
3. Blue Team reveals **Gateway** or **SCADA Web Interface**
4. Blue Team may respond with **Port Closure**
5. Facilitator explains: this reduces exposed services

The table learns the cyber concept through the card interaction.

---

# Slide 15 — What Blue Team Should Think About

Blue Team should ask:

- Which asset is most critical right now?
- Can we detect what Red Team is doing?
- Do we have a defense card for this attack?
- Can we recover if something is damaged?
- What should we reinforce before the next attack?

Blue Team is not just blocking attacks. Blue Team is keeping water operations running.

---

# Slide 16 — What Red Team Should Think About

Red Team should ask:

- What can we learn through recon?
- Which asset is exposed?
- Can we use credentials, phishing, or public exploits?
- Can we establish access?
- Can we cause operational impact?

Red Team should feel realistic, not magical.

# Slide 17 — 2-Hour Session Agenda

| Time | Activity |
|---:|---|
| 0-10 min | Welcome and story setup |
| 10-20 min | Explain teams and objective |
| 20-30 min | Explain card anatomy |
| 30-40 min | Guided sample turn |
| 40-70 min | Play Round 1 with facilitator help |
| 70-80 min | Debrief Round 1 |
| 80-105 min | Play Round 2 faster |
| 105-115 min | Debrief Round 2 |
| 115-120 min | Closing feedback |

---

# Slide 18 — Facilitator Rule

Do not teach this like a complex card game.

Teach it like a tabletop exercise:

- Explain the story
- Let players make decisions
- Pause to connect cards to real-world lessons
- Keep the game moving
- Debrief after each round

The facilitator makes the game educational.

---

# Slide 19 — Debrief Questions

After each round, ask:

- What did Red Team do first?
- Which asset felt most important?
- Which defense helped the most?
- Did the attack feel realistic?
- What would this look like at a real utility?
- What card was confusing?
- What card taught you something useful?

---

# Slide 20 — What Success Looks Like

The session is successful if players can explain:

- Why exposed services matter
- Why MFA helps
- Why port closure helps
- Why operator laptops and gateways matter
- Why vendors and recovery planning matter
- How a cyber issue can become an operational water-sector issue

Fun matters because people remember what they played.

---

# Slide 21 — Why AWWA Should Care

This game helps water-sector teams practice cybersecurity conversations without starting from fear or jargon.

It gives operators, IT, OT, and leadership a shared language.

Cyber Realm ICS-WATER is:

- Practical
- Scenario-driven
- Easy to facilitate
- Repeatable
- More engaging than a lecture

# Slide 22 — Closing Message

Cyber resilience is not only a technical problem.

Cyber Realm ICS-WATER helps teams practice the decisions that keep water systems running.
